The Catcher in the Rye

by J.D. Salinger

Rating: 4.3/5

Holden Caulfield recounts his experiences over a few days in New York City, grappling with isolation, loss, and identity. The novel captures teenage rebellion and the struggle to find one's place in a conformist society. Salinger's iconic work remains a landmark in coming-of-age literature.
